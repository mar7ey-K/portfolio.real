Green Bean Casserole Recipe

History

In 1955, a woman of the name Dorcas Reilly worked as a supervisor in the home economics division at Campbell's Headquarters in Camden, New Jersey. There, she was asked to create a dish that would be featured in the Associated Press. The recipe was based on ingredients you had around the house, and this would include the Campbell mushroom soup and green beans. Dorcas, and her team, toyed with many ingredients, including celery or ham, but ended up on the Green Bean Casserole we know today, it was cheap, took little time to make, and helped with the more and more women joining the work-force because there was no one else at home to create such complex meals. Dorcas originally called this dish the Green Bean Bake, and was surprised how much of a hit it was! This recipe is printed onto the Campbell's mushroom soup can and is made during Thanksgiving by millions of Americans.

Ingredients

  • 1 can Campbell's Cream of Mushroom Soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspon soy sauce
  • 4 cups cooked, uncut, green beans
  • 1 and 1/3 French's French Fried Onions

Directions

  1. Heat your oven to 350Â°F. Stir the soup, milk, soy sauce, beans, and 2/3 onions into a 1 and 1/2 quart casserole dish. You can then season the mixture with salt and pepper.
  2. Bake the dish for 25 minutes, or until hot. Stir the mixture and add in the last 2/3 cup of onions.
  3. Bake for another 5 minutes, or until the onions are a golden brown.
